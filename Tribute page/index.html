Who is Sundar Pichai?

  • Sundar Pichai is the current CEO of Google and Alphabet. Known as a successful innovator, he focuses on developing new products and services at Google.

  • Sundar Pichai was born on June 10, 1972, in Madurai, India, and studied engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology in Kharagpur.

  • He earned a master's degree from Stanford University and an MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.

  • Pichai's early experience includes positions at Allied Materials and McKinsey & Co.

Gaining Experience

  • In 1997, Sundar Pichai abandoned the Ph.D. program at Stanford University, accepting his master's degree in material sciences and engineering.

  • He joined Allied Materials, a semiconductor manufacturer in Silicon Valley, as a product manager before leaving to pursue an MBA at the University of Pennsylvania.

  • After graduation in 2002, Pichai accepted a position as a management consultant at McKinsey & Co.

  • Having roles of both product manager and consultant at two leading firms, which require product strategy, execution, and team building, prepared Sundar Pichai for his new position at Google in 2004.


Success at Google

  • Sundar Pichai's interview at Google coincidently coincided with the launch of Google Gmail.

  • Unaware of the new product, Pichai admitted his ignorance during his interviews and is remembered as the candidate with "intellectual humility," which Laszlo Bock, Google’s former senior VP of people operations, called one of the top qualities he looked for in candidates at Google.

  • Pichai was well-prepared but managed a dynamic situation with candor during a moment of uncertainty

Sundar Pichai's Significant Success

  • Sundar Pichai's career trajectory demonstrates his remarkable talent, leadership abilities, and strategic vision.

  • Pichai's leadership and strategic ideas drew the attention of Google's senior management, resulting in his swift rise through the ranks.

  • In 2013, he was named Senior Vice President of Android, Google's mobile operating system, where he supervised Android's development and expansion into a worldwide platform with billions of users.
